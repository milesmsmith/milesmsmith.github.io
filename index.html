ACTIVEcharge

Prototype designs of a Piezoelectric Energy Harvesting System

ACTIVEcharge is a piezoelectric energy harvesting system currently in development that originated from research conducted in the ED Lab at UMBC. The purpose of ACTIVEcharge is to reduce the cost to replace structural health monitoring sensors in wind turbines to reduce the overall operation and maintenance costs associated with wind energy and promote the commercial viability of wind energy.

ACTIVEcharge aimed to determine if enough power could be gathered through a piezoelectric energy harvesting system. The fundamental theory that guided this research is the piezoelectric effect, which is the ability of certain materials to generate an electric charge from mechanical stress. The ambient energy from the rotation of a wind turbine was used to power the energy harvesting system by indirectly applying stress to a piezoelectric material creating voltage and electrically converted to harnessable power.

My involvement in the project began in the early phases of the project. Throughout the timeline of the project I had involvement in the development of this project included researching and developing non-destructive techniques for harnessing the rotational energy of a wind turbine, designing and manufacturing the prototype, and testing and power optimization of the energy harvesting system. During this time period, we transitioned from a contact-driven technique for applying stress on the piezoelectric material to a non-contact technique that utilized magnetic forces. We also developed and tested a functional prototype to provide structural and funcitonal analysis. As a result of this study we were able to further the research and begin development of the current product, ACTIVEcharge.
