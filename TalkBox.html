TalkBox

The TalkBox is an interactive communication device for non-verbal individuals started by Dr. Foad Hamidi of the UMBC Information Systems department. The goal of the TalkBox is to develop an open-source system that is accessible to younger individuals and facilitates introductory levels of communication.

As part of a team of mechanical engineers, I worked to make the TalkBox more modular. This was primarily done by separating the delicate electrical components and the user interface. The main challenge was securing and protecting the electrical components (Raspberry Pi, Capacative HAT Sensor, Battery, Speaker) from water damage and developing a system that secures the TalkBox together after separation of the electrical components and user interface.
