Piezoelectric Materials

While at UMBC, I studied piezoelectric materials which are nifty materials that can convert mechanical strain into electrical energy via the piezoelectric effect. While not capable of transforming how we produce energy, piezoelectrics can be allowed to low power applications. For example, we developed an energy harvesting system that could harness the rotational kinetic energy from a wind turbine to apply load to the piezo material so that we could power a wind turbine's wireless sensor network (for structural health monitoring applications)!
